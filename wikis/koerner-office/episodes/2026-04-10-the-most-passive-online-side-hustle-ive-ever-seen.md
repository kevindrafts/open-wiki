## Source summary

Chris demonstrates building a simple calculator website with AI/vibe-coding tools. The thesis is that narrow calculators, quizzes, and generators can attract search traffic, capture leads, or monetize with ads/affiliate/lead-sale paths because they solve one searchable problem.

## Business ideas

- Niche calculator sites for side-hustle profit, AI costs, pet cremation, or industry-specific pricing.
- Lead-generation calculators sold to local/specialty providers.
- SEO pages around calculator outputs and tips.
- Replicable calculator templates for multiple niches.

## Customer segments mentioned

- consumers searching for a price estimate
- veterinarians and pet/horse cremation providers buying leads
- AI builders estimating token/API costs
- side-hustlers checking unit economics
- niche operators with expensive or hard-to-explain pricing

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Omni Calculator is cited as a large-scale example with thousands of calculators and heavy traffic.
- A calculator can monetize through ads, email capture, affiliate offers, or selling leads to providers.
- The build cost is framed as minutes/hours with AI tools, but traffic and conversion remain the hard parts.

## Acquisition channels

- SEO from searchable calculator queries
- long-tail pages and usage tips
- email unlock for results
- lead sale to vendors in the niche

## Operational steps

1. Brainstorm problems where users search for a number or estimate.
2. Check whether search demand exists before building.
3. Build one calculator with clear inputs and an immediately useful output.
4. Add explanatory tips to improve SEO and trust.
5. Capture email or lead details only after showing value.
6. Copy the template into adjacent niches if early demand appears.

## Tools / AI workflows

- Hostinger/vibe-coding style website builder
- AI prompts to generate calculator logic, copy, and SEO content
- token estimator field for AI-cost calculator concept
- simple email capture/unlock flow

## Caveats and claims to verify

- “Passive” depends on ranking, lead demand, and maintenance.
- Calculators that estimate regulated/financial/medical costs need disclaimers.
- Thin AI-generated calculators may not rank unless they are genuinely useful.
