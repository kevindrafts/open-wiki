## Source summary

Michael explains how a passion for pizza became a packaged pizza-dough-mix brand validated through farmers markets and then scaled online. Koerner also opens the episode by describing farmers markets as a low-overhead platform both for organizing markets and for testing physical products.

## Business ideas

- Sell packaged pizza dough mix at farmers markets.
- Use live demos/sampling to create content and customer proof.
- Move winning farmers-market products online with paid social.
- Organize vendor markets by renting/partnering around existing foot traffic.

## Customer segments mentioned

- home pizza makers
- farmers-market shoppers
- golfers/event attendees who enjoy food demos
- people who want artisan food without restaurant-level equipment
- other food-product founders validating demand

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Guest says first summer farmers markets sold about $60K working under 20 hours/week.
- First three months were around $20K/month.
- Online sales reportedly surpassed four months of market sales in under 30 days.
- Best month reported over $160K in online sales.
- Ingredient margins discussed as high, with Koerner guessing around 80% on dry mix.

## Acquisition channels

- farmers markets and city markets
- live pizza samples/demos
- user-generated content from market interactions
- Facebook/Instagram/Google ads after validation
- branded bags and packaging

## Operational steps

1. Choose a product tied to genuine expertise/passion.
2. Package it in a shelf-stable, demonstrable format.
3. Attend multiple markets weekly to test demand and messaging.
4. Sample the product and capture customer reactions/content.
5. Track which market types perform best.
6. Use learning and proof to run online ads and scale ecommerce.

## Tools / AI workflows

- gram scales, sealer, gusset bags, dry ingredients
- small pizza oven for demos
- cameras behind booth for content
- Facebook/Instagram/Google ads

## Caveats and claims to verify

- Farmers markets vary widely; city-run markets performed better than low-traffic roadside markets in the guest's experience.
- Food licensing, cottage-food rules, and packaging requirements vary by location.
- Paid ad performance improved only after the founder learned it directly.
