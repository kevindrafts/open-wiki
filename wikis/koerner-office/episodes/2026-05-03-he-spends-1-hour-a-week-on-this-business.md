## Source summary

A local operator describes a nearly self-serve indoor golf simulator membership business. The model uses recurring memberships, 24/7 access, scheduling software, cameras, and a small local member base rather than high walk-in traffic.

## Business ideas

- Indoor golf simulator membership club.
- Punch cards for occasional users.
- Multi-location membership reciprocity.
- Potential franchise path after several proven locations.

## Customer segments mentioned

- golfers without home simulators
- parents/teen players practicing nearby
- neighbors within a few miles
- small local golf communities and friends-of-friends

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Membership tiers: $175/month for four tee times, $275/month for eight, $325/month unlimited.
- Break-even within about three months.
- Target model mentioned: about 40 members, $100K revenue, roughly 50%-55% net margins.
- Scheduling software cost mentioned around $40/month.

## Acquisition channels

- friends/network seed members
- frontage/signage near neighborhoods
- word of mouth and member referrals
- trial/feedback from early members before grand opening
- location convenience rather than mass foot traffic

## Operational steps

1. Lease a small neighborhood-accessible space with visible frontage.
2. Install simulator, cameras, access control, and scheduling.
3. Recruit initial members from personal network for feedback and baseline revenue.
4. Offer tiered recurring memberships plus punch cards.
5. Keep operations self-serve with 24/7 door access and periodic cleaning.
6. Delay heavy marketing until demand and operations are understood.

## Tools / AI workflows

- scheduling software
- 24/7 door access
- video surveillance/cameras
- golf simulator hardware and data systems

## Caveats and claims to verify

- Leasehold improvements, permits, insurance, and simulator capex can materially affect returns.
- The operator had useful network advantages including legal/real-estate help.
- Local demand may vary; the model needs enough golfers near the location.
