## Summary

This episode profiles Colin, a credit-card rewards consultant who helps business owners earn and redeem points from spending they already do. The business is a high-margin, expertise-and-content model: learn the points ecosystem, publish useful examples, charge flat fees for strategy calls and award-travel research, and use AI/tools as research leverage while keeping a human advisory layer.

## Concrete business ideas

### Credit-card rewards strategy for business owners

Help small-business owners choose cards and spending workflows that earn more points or cash-equivalent rewards from existing expenses. Colin describes a flat-fee consulting package for reviewing personal/business spending and building a card strategy.

Revenue clue from the episode: Colin says he charges $875 for a one-time package.

### Points redemption / award-travel research

Offer research for people who already have points but do not know how to redeem them effectively. The work includes identifying viable airline/hotel transfer partners, searching award availability, and planning trips such as business-class flights or luxury hotel redemptions.

Revenue clue from the episode: Colin says he charges flat fees for award research, and mentions a recent month with roughly $6,000 in total revenue from a mix of strategy and research work.

### Affiliate-supported advisory

Use card referral/affiliate links where appropriate, while still recommending based on the client's needs. Colin says card affiliate commissions are a meaningful part of revenue, but the episode also implies this needs trust and disclosure discipline.

### Beginner “zero-to-one” points help

For a simpler side hustle, help one local business owner move from a low-reward 1% cash-back card to a more suitable rewards setup. Koerner and Colin discuss that someone can learn enough from blogs and AI tools to help a beginner, especially if they start with lower-ticket help instead of complex redemptions.

## Customer segments

- small-business owners with meaningful monthly card spend
- local businesses still using low-reward bank cards
- entrepreneurs with large ad spend, shipping, inventory, or operating expenses
- families or business owners sitting on hundreds of thousands/millions of points
- people who want premium flights or hotels but do not want to learn award search
- points beginners who want a human to confirm a strategy

## Tools and research workflow

- Claude / ChatGPT / Gemini for learning and research assistance
- points blogs and newsletters such as Frequent Miler, The Points Guy, Daily Drop, and 10xTravel
- award-search tools such as seats.aero
- newsletter/content archives connected to AI tools for querying prior examples
- manual review and booking guidance for real client trips

## Operational playbook

1. Learn the basics of major flexible-points ecosystems, transfer partners, common categories, and pitfalls.
2. Start with a narrow customer: business owners with significant monthly expenses.
3. Audit current card setup, spending categories, goals, and risk tolerance.
4. Recommend a simple strategy that avoids unnecessary card complexity.
5. Publish case studies showing points earned or trips found.
6. Build an inbound channel through LinkedIn or email rather than relying only on outbound.
7. Use AI and award-search tools for research, but keep human judgment for feasibility and client trust.
8. Add contractors only after the research workload becomes repeatable.

## Caveats / claims to verify

- Credit-card recommendations can involve affiliate incentives, consumer-finance rules, credit-score impacts, annual fees, and tax/accounting questions.
- Points are not cash; programs devalue and award availability changes quickly.
- “Earn and burn” is emphasized because holding points can lose value through inflation/devaluation.
- AI can suggest impossible redemptions; human verification is required before advising clients.
- Any public page based on this should avoid personalized financial advice.
