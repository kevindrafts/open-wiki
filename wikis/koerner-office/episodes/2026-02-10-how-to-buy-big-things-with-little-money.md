## Summary

This episode compiles [[creative-seller-financing]] tactics for buying businesses, real estate, equipment, franchises, or equity when cash is limited. Koerner's core argument is that trust and value creation matter more than clever terms: operators can use public proof, relationship-building, seller financing, graduated payments, partial seller carrybacks, master leases, subject-to debt, collateral, and delayed down payments when the asset math supports the risk.

## Concrete business ideas

- **Business acquisition with seller financing:** buy a local service business, improve marketing, then refinance or resell.
- **Value-add real estate acquisition:** use seller terms or carrybacks when the seller believes in post-close improvements.
- **Master lease / lease-to-own:** control an underperforming asset and earn the option to buy after improving operations.
- **Subject-to acquisition:** take over payments while existing debt remains in place, where legal and acceptable.
- **Public build-in-public funding:** use public proof to earn trust, partners, vendors, or financing.

## Customer segments

Asset sellers, tired business owners, real estate sellers, lenders, potential partners, vendors, franchise sellers, and operators with skills but limited capital.

## Acquisition channels mentioned or implied

Public storytelling, short-form video proof, relationship-building, seller conversations, value-add plans, creative terms, and reputation risk.

## Operational playbook

1. Do enough research to understand the asset's real risk and upside.
2. Build trust before proposing aggressive terms.
3. Offer value, show competence, and risk reputation before asking for concessions.
4. Match structure to the seller's constraint: cash, monthly income, risk, taxes, or future upside.
5. Keep emotion out of the deal and be willing to walk.

## Caveats / claims to verify

Creative finance can create legal, tax, lender-consent, due-on-sale, securities, foreclosure, and personal-guarantee risks. Use lawyers, accountants, and lenders before executing any structure.
