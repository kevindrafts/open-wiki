## Source summary

Michael Girdley and Chris work through several business ideas: marketplaces for AI-agent personas, local investigative media, curated feeds/newsletters, AI-personalized skincare, productivity-lock apps, college-tuition negotiation services, and AI audits for small businesses. The strongest recurring theme is using AI and focused distribution to turn expert workflows into lightweight products or services.

## Business ideas

- Marketplace for reusable AI agents/personas/skills.
- Paid local journalism or deep local business/civic reporting aided by AI research.
- Curated “best of the internet” feed/newsletter or physical digest.
- AI-assisted skincare consultation and subscription formulation.
- Productivity-lock app that blocks task switching until a stated task is done.
- College-tuition negotiation service for parents.
- AI audits for SMBs that become implementation projects.

## Customer segments mentioned

- businesses buying deployable AI agents
- local readers underserved by thin city coverage
- parents negotiating college pricing
- skincare customers seeking personalized routines
- productivity/self-control buyers
- SMB owners confused about where AI fits

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Girdley frames some laptop-first ideas as capable of $100K-$200K/year with a small client base.
- College negotiation could charge a flat fee, percentage of savings, or hybrid.
- AI audits should be paid when they require real work, with implementation as the larger upside.
- Niche newsletters can monetize with paid subscriptions, cohorts, ads, and sponsorships.

## Acquisition channels

- focused niche newsletter/content distribution
- local ads near affluent high schools for tuition negotiation
- city/community demand for deeper local reporting
- social proof and creator-led distribution
- audits sold into SMB confusion around AI roadmaps

## Operational steps

1. Pick a narrow buyer and pain before building.
2. Use AI to research, draft, and operationalize expert workflows.
3. Package the output as a paid product, marketplace listing, subscription, or service.
4. Use a direct niche channel rather than broad consumer marketing.
5. For AI audits, diagnose the roadmap first, then sell the fix.

## Tools / AI workflows

- AI-agent/skill marketplaces
- ChatGPT/Claude data exports and persona training concepts
- AI research for local journalism and college negotiation scripts
- AI image/selfie analysis for skincare concepts
- Vibe coding for small apps and prototypes

## Caveats and claims to verify

- Persona/celebrity bots raise rights, likeness, and source-data issues.
- Local journalism still needs human reporting and verification.
- Skincare recommendations can involve health/regulatory claims.
- Tuition-negotiation results depend on school incentives and financial-aid rules.
