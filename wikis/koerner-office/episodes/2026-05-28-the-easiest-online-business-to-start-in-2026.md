## Summary

This episode breaks down [[white-label-vertical-saas]] businesses built on top of GoHighLevel and adjacent tools. The core play is to pick a narrow niche, reskin or package existing software, sell a specific painkiller, and use monthly pricing rather than fragile lifetime deals.

## Concrete business ideas

- Notary software / training bundle for mobile notaries
- Gym check-in and gym management software
- Tutor or educator operations software
- Insurance-agent CRM, training, community, and sales-tool bundle
- Tools sold to other GoHighLevel agencies, not just end customers
- Country- or language-specific CRM clone for underserved markets

## Customer segments

- mobile notaries
- gyms and fitness studios
- tutors, educators, and course sellers
- insurance agents selling complex products
- GoHighLevel agencies
- entrepreneurs in non-US or non-English markets with few localized CRM options
- niche creators or trainers with trusted audiences

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Notary example: $97/month or about $1,000/year; Koerner infers at least $100,000/month from a claimed 1,000+ customer tier.
- Gym example: $229/month plus $700 setup fee, with higher plans around $550-$600/month.
- Insurance bundle example: $500 for 90 days, then $180/month.
- Agency-tool example: $497/month unlimited tier; Koerner frames 1,000 customers as a possible $500,000/month run-rate.
- International CRM examples: roughly $97-$149/month.
- Warning example: one-time/lifetime pricing can fail if support and platform costs continue monthly.

## Acquisition channels

- Partner with micro-influencers or niche experts and split revenue.
- DM prospects or creators in lower-competition channels.
- Use G2 reviews to identify missing features in niche software.
- Search YouTube for niche + training / academy / certification to find educators with audiences.
- Join GoHighLevel Facebook groups and Slack communities when selling add-ons to agencies.
- Use founder/contact databases and tech-stack tools for targeted outreach.

## Operational playbook

1. Pick a niche where buyers already pay for software, training, or operations help.
2. Identify one painful workflow instead of building a broad platform first.
3. Package existing software, snapshots, templates, training, community, or automations.
4. Sell the wedge feature hard; expand later into upsells and ecosystem lock-in.
5. Price monthly so revenue covers platform fees, support, ads, and operator time.
6. Partner with someone who has niche trust if you lack the audience.
7. Reverse-engineer competitors' sites, reviews, founders, and tech stacks before building.

## AI/tool workflow

- GoHighLevel / HighLevel for CRM, funnels, memberships, courses, payments, and white-label SaaS
- ChatGPT/Claude for review analysis, feature-gap discovery, and snapshot planning
- G2 and Trustpilot for customer pain mining
- Wayback Machine / Internet Archive for offer and website history
- Wappalyzer, publicwww, PredictLeads, Apollo, and TheirStack for competitive research and outreach

## Caveats / claims to verify

- Revenue estimates are inferred from award tiers and public pricing, not verified financial statements.
- White-label offers must avoid trademark infringement and misleading positioning.
- Lifetime pricing can create support and platform-cost liabilities if there is no recurring revenue.
- Some niches, especially insurance, may require licensing/compliance review.
- International localization requires language, payment, support, and legal nuance; translation alone is not enough.
