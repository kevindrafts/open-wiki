## Summary

This episode frames a reportedly restricted Anthropic model as a market signal for future long-running AI agents: if governments treat a model like strategic infrastructure, Koerner argues entrepreneurs should prepare business models that become more valuable when similar agentic systems return. The practical ideas are mostly [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]]: legacy-software migration, bookkeeping cleanup, productized one-person agencies, hyper-personalized outreach, and self-improving digital-asset portfolios.

## Concrete business ideas

### Legacy software migration rescue

Help companies move from outdated internal tools, desktop accounting systems, old CRMs, or legacy codebases to modern software. The promise is a narrow, outcome-based migration with minimal downtime. The AI angle is using agents to inspect old systems, map dependencies, generate migration scripts, test output, and create documentation.

Related pages: [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]], [[boring-business-ideas]], [[sales-and-distribution-playbooks]].

### Overnight bookkeeping cleanup

Offer fast cleanup for messy SMB books: unreconciled bank statements, spreadsheets, invoices, receipts, and transaction categories. AI can categorize, reconcile, flag exceptions, and produce cleanup notes; a human still reviews edge cases before delivery.

Revenue clue from the episode: cleanup jobs are described as potentially charging $3,500-$10,000, with a beginner model of roughly one $3,000 cleanup job per week.

Related pages: [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]], [[one-person-businesses]].

### Productized one-person agency

Use a DesignJoy-style model: one clear service, one active request at a time, monthly subscription, and AI-assisted fulfillment behind the scenes. Candidate services include landing pages, ad creative, email campaigns, website updates, and content repurposing.

Revenue clue from the episode: productized agency subscriptions are framed around thousands per month per client.

Related pages: [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]], [[sales-and-distribution-playbooks]], [[one-person-businesses]].

### Hyper-personalized outreach and list monetization

Use AI to enrich prospect/customer lists and generate more specific cold emails or reactivation campaigns. Example customers include gyms, med spas, and local service businesses with underused email lists.

Related pages: [[local-service-lead-generation]], [[sales-and-distribution-playbooks]], [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]].

### Self-improving digital asset portfolios

Build and maintain niche sites, directories, lead-gen sites, or “website landlord” assets that AI can monitor and improve over time using analytics and search-console data.

Revenue clue from the episode: small digital assets are described as potentially producing hundreds to low thousands per month each.

Related pages: [[tiny-tools-and-micro-apps]], [[one-person-businesses]], [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]].

## Operational playbook

1. Pick one narrow customer and one painful workflow.
2. Package the offer around a concrete outcome, not “AI consulting.”
3. Build a simple landing page and manual fulfillment workflow first.
4. Use current AI models to prototype the process.
5. Keep human review in the loop, especially for financial, migration, or customer-facing work.
6. Avoid dependence on one model/provider.
7. Replace manual steps with stronger autonomous agents as they become reliable.
