## Source summary

William Lindholm describes a corporate outbound gifting company that sends cakes and other physical objects to prospects as a way to cut through AI-saturated digital outreach. The company coordinates local vendors, tracks deliveries, and sells attention/meeting conversion rather than the cake itself.

## Business ideas

- Send cakes/physical gifts to sales prospects to book meetings.
- Coordinate local bakeries and vendors for multi-city campaigns.
- Expand from cakes into other high-attention physical experiences.
- Integrate gifting campaigns with HubSpot/Salesforce for recurring enterprise workflows.

## Customer segments mentioned

- B2B sales teams
- enterprise SaaS companies
- companies with high customer lifetime value
- founders/teams trying to win attention in crowded outbound channels
- local bakeries/vendors as fulfillment partners

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Company reportedly reached $120K/month within six months.
- Norway birthday-gift business produced passive invoicing around $12K/month.
- Guest claims cakes converted around 35% of recipients into meetings in early data.
- Some customers moved toward recurring campaigns/integrations.

## Acquisition channels

- outbound sales teams using physical gifts before/after cold outreach
- recipients posting gifts on social media, creating PR and inbound demand
- recurring enterprise campaigns
- personal LinkedIn/Instagram presence of founder

## Operational steps

1. Identify high-value prospects where a meeting is worth the gift cost.
2. Send a personalized cake/physical item to the prospect or office.
3. Track delivery with photos/status from local vendors.
4. Follow up with a sales pitch tied to the gift.
5. Measure meeting and close-rate lift versus normal outbound.
6. Integrate recurring campaigns with CRM systems as volume grows.

## Tools / AI workflows

- local bakery/vendor network
- delivery tracking and photos
- HubSpot/Salesforce integrations requested by customers
- AI agents as the macro reason digital outreach gets noisier

## Caveats and claims to verify

- Novel gifting channels can saturate; the guest explicitly expects cakes to become overused.
- Delivery logistics, quality control, payment terms, and vendor reliability are core risks.
- Meeting-conversion and revenue numbers are self-reported.
- Gift compliance/ethics policies vary by recipient company and industry.
