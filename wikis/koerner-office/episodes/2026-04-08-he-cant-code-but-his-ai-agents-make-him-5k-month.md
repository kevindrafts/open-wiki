## Source summary

An 18-year-old founder explains how he vibe-coded an AI clipping/scheduling SaaS without traditional coding experience, reached early paid revenue through X/build-in-public distribution, and uses AI agents as a functional team for coding, marketing, growth, and research.

## Business ideas

- AI clipping SaaS for creators, podcasters, and short-form repurposing.
- Agentic clipping workflow that finds campaigns, clips videos, adds assets, schedules posts, and submits links.
- Build-in-public SaaS using X as the first distribution channel.
- Agent-run “company” structure with specialized CEO, coding, marketing, and growth agents.

## Customer segments mentioned

- podcasters and creators repurposing long-form into short-form
- clipping agencies and clippers
- streamers and creator campaigns
- users searching for alternatives to Opus Clip and similar tools
- technical and nontechnical founders using AI coding tools

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Paid tiers mentioned at $9, $39, and $99/month.
- Guest reports $5K revenue in the prior 30 days and around 80%-85% margins.
- Removing the free plan helped reduce credit abuse; a $9 floor filtered for serious users.
- API credit usage is a key cost driver.

## Acquisition channels

- X build-in-public posts
- OpenClaw/Hermes-style agents finding complaints about competitor tools
- DMs to users discussing clipping, AI video tools, or outages
- short-form content expansion beyond X
- beta users providing feedback before paid conversion

## Operational steps

1. Research competitors deeply before building.
2. Start with AI coding tools, but provide precise context and screenshots.
3. Launch early enough to collect real users, even before analytics are perfect.
4. Use agents to find competitor-pain posts and invite users to try the product.
5. Instrument analytics once usage appears.
6. Raise the floor from free to low-paid if free users consume too many credits.

## Tools / AI workflows

- Lovable, Claude Code, Codex, Gemini API, Supabase, Vercel
- PostHog analytics and event tracking
- Hermes Agent / agent teams for research, growth, marketing, and coding
- Paperclip-style AI orchestration concepts
- Miro or mind mapping for market research

## Caveats and claims to verify

- No-code/vibe-coded SaaS still requires debugging, cost controls, and user analytics.
- Free plans can burn API credits without creating buyers.
- Clipping depends on platform rules, copyrighted inputs, and creator authorization.
- Agent memory and autonomy claims should be tested per workflow.
