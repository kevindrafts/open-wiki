## Source summary

Josh explains a local co-op postcard business: sell ad spaces to non-competing local businesses, use USPS Every Door Direct Mail to target selected routes, design the shared card, and mail it to homeowners. The model validates cheaply and sells mostly through Facebook groups and text/DM.

## Business ideas

- Shared postcard mailers for local home-service and brick-and-mortar businesses.
- Smaller recurring community cards for restaurants, bakeries, and local retail.
- Solo cards for individual advertisers.
- Upsells into websites, QR-code landing pages, SEO, Google/Facebook packages, and tracking.

## Customer segments mentioned

- landscapers, roofers, plumbers, junk haulers, carpet/window cleaners
- bakeries and restaurants with local offers
- realtors and home-value lead magnets
- permanent holiday-lighting and other high-ticket local services
- businesses already buying yard signs or physical ads

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Example 10,000-card run: about $2,500 postage plus roughly $2,300 printing.
- Sample card spaces discussed at $600, $999, $250-$300, and $497 depending on size/run.
- Guest reported around $50K in a year from a $90 start, with roughly half as profit in the early business.
- Solo cards had lower margins but less coordination work.

## Acquisition channels

- local/business Facebook groups
- short posts emphasizing “one per industry” and “first come first serve”
- DMs and text follow-up; guest says most sales close without calls
- driving around for yard signs and physical advertisers
- cold calls, business walk-ins, and referrals from advertisers

## Operational steps

1. Pick routes with USPS EDDM, filtering for income and housing fit.
2. Match advertiser categories to the target area.
3. Sell enough front-side inventory to cover print/postage costs.
4. Design ads in Canva or similar tools and include dynamic QR codes.
5. Print on quality cardstock; confirm bundling/USPS requirements.
6. Mail the cards and collect advertiser follow-up/case studies.
7. Re-offer first right of refusal to previous advertisers for nearby routes.

## Tools / AI workflows

- USPS Every Door Direct Mail tool
- Canva or similar ad layout tools
- dynamic QR-code tracking
- Facebook groups and Messenger
- scraping/direct-mail-to-advertisers idea for scaling sponsor acquisition

## Caveats and claims to verify

- The service sells exposure, not guaranteed ROI.
- Weak advertiser offers and poor tracking make results hard to prove.
- Postal bundling and EDDM rules matter operationally.
- Follow-up case studies are a key missing system in the guest’s current workflow.
