## Source summary

Kip explains live-selling liquidation goods on Whatnot and TikTok Shop. The model buys liquidated or overstock products with math, then sells them through energetic live auctions where perceived value, community, and showmanship can create high profit per hour.

## Business ideas

- Whatnot live auctions for liquidation appliances, food, fashion, footwear, or unique lots.
- TikTok Shop live selling for higher viewership but lower buyer intent.
- Streamer-led liquidation brand with employees hosting shows.
- Newsletter/content teaching live-selling operators.

## Customer segments mentioned

- Whatnot and TikTok live-shopping buyers
- liquidation suppliers and B-Stock-style sources
- customers seeking deals on high perceived value items
- entrepreneurs willing to host energetic livestreams
- streamers/operators paid to run shows

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Guest reports $450K last year after platform fees and $45K in the last 30 days.
- Profit-per-hour examples include $1,200-$1,500/hour and a first house livestream of $900 in 90 minutes.
- Typical stream goal is around four hours.
- One TikTok example: soccer jerseys cost about $12 and produced roughly $10 profit/unit, with about $2K profit in one stream.

## Acquisition channels

- Whatnot discovery/swipe feed can send viewers to new sellers.
- TikTok live has larger audience but lower purchase intent.
- Building a community/follower base compounds over time.
- Cross-category tests use the existing audience if trust is strong.

## Operational steps

1. Watch streams to study format, products, pricing, and why buyers engage.
2. Source liquidation/overstock where math leaves margin at auction prices.
3. Prefer products with high perceived value and easy testing/verification.
4. Run high-energy streams and start auctions low enough to create action.
5. Track profit per hour after goods, platform fees, labor, and packing.
6. Hire/compensate streamers once shows repeat.
7. Solve fulfillment and shipping objections before scaling.

## Tools / AI workflows

- Whatnot and TikTok Shop live-selling platforms
- integrated platform shipping labels
- B-Stock/liquidation supplier research
- product math and sell-through tracking
- content/newsletter around live-selling lessons

## Caveats and claims to verify

- Buying liquidation without math can destroy margins.
- Returns/defects/testing vary heavily by product category.
- Live selling requires temperament, energy, and customer trust.
- Platform dependency is high; Whatnot and TikTok audiences behave differently.
