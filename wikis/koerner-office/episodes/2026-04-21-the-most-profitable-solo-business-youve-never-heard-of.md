## Source summary

Brett Williams explains DesignJoy, a one-person productized design subscription business. The reusable pattern is a narrow service packaged as a subscription with clear throughput, pause/resume flexibility, low overhead, and speed-to-value for companies that do not want agency friction.

## Business ideas

- Productized design subscription for startups and software companies.
- AI-first design/development service that helps nontechnical customers ship apps.
- Productized copywriting or short-form clipping agencies.
- Support layer around AI/vibe-coded products: database, Stripe, hosting, polish, launch.

## Customer segments mentioned

- software companies and startups
- founders needing landing pages, brand, product, or web design
- nontechnical people stuck after vibe-coding prototypes
- creators needing clip/content repurposing

## Pricing / revenue clues

- DesignJoy reportedly does about $1M/year solo.
- Monthly recurring revenue often averages $70K-$80K/month, with peaks/troughs.
- Expenses outside taxes described around $1K/month.
- Founder kept full-time job until the side business reached about $80K/month.

## Acquisition channels

- Product Hunt launch drove the initial wave.
- clear subscription landing page and productized offer
- reputation/proof from finished work
- recurring subscriptions with pause/resume flexibility
- low-friction purchase compared with agency scoping

## Operational steps

1. Pick a service where speed and recurring demand matter.
2. Package the deliverable as a subscription rather than custom proposals.
3. Keep scope narrow enough for one person to fulfill.
4. Use pause/resume to reduce buyer anxiety and churn conflict.
5. Use AI to speed requirements, UX flows, concepts, and prompt-writing.
6. Avoid overstaffing; preserve high margins if the bottleneck is personal skill.

## Tools / AI workflows

- Claude/ChatGPT for requirements and UX thinking
- Midjourney and Krea for image/video generation
- Figma for design work
- screenshots/reference prompting for AI-assisted layouts
- AI prompt-writing by AI itself

## Caveats and claims to verify

- Solo scale depends on founder skill, speed, and taste.
- MRR can swing materially due to churn and paused subscriptions.
- AI can accelerate design but does not fully replace high-quality taste/branding.
- Revenue figures are self-reported.
