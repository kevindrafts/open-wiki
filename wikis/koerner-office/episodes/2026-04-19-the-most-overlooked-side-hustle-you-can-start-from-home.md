## Source summary

Rayma describes a home-started corporate gifting business that grew through referrals and LinkedIn rather than ads. The business bundles sourcing, personalization, packing, and shipping for companies that want memorable client/prospect/team gifts without handling logistics themselves.

## Business ideas

- Corporate gifting service for prospects, clients, and internal teams.
- Personalized/alma-mater/hobby gift boxes for sales outreach.
- Branded team survival kits and seasonal gifts.
- In-house personalization using a laser engraver.

## Customer segments mentioned

- sales teams and founders
- companies sending prospecting gifts
- teams buying employee/internal gifts
- busy professionals who value outsourcing logistics
- stay-at-home parents seeking flexible fulfillment work

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Prior year revenue reported at $357K while working around 20 hours/week.
- Average prospecting gift example around $100.
- Starting framework mentions $50 gifts, 50% gross profit, minimums of 10 gifts, and roughly $50/hour early work.
- Current minimums: no gifts under $50 and no orders under 10.

## Acquisition channels

- referrals and word of mouth
- LinkedIn posts from clients showing gifts
- networking and fast responses
- social proof from high-quality unboxing experiences
- direct wholesale/vendor relationships

## Operational steps

1. Start with personal/local gifting and move into corporate use cases.
2. Build gift concepts around client goals, recipient details, and budget.
3. Source from wholesale platforms or direct vendor programs.
4. Personalize boxes, bags, inserts, and items where useful.
5. Pack/ship efficiently and charge for outsourced convenience.
6. Set minimum order sizes so small jobs remain profitable.

## Tools / AI workflows

- Faire and direct wholesale programs
- laser engraver for personalization
- branded boxes/bags and crinkle paper
- ShipStation, Shopify shipping rates, Pirate Ship, Stamps.com, FedEx/UPS reps

## Caveats and claims to verify

- Shipping, filler, boxes, and expedited international sourcing can erode margins.
- Wholesale access may require proper business setup/EIN.
- Deadlines and bad-fit clients need firm boundaries.
- Gift compliance policies may affect prospecting campaigns.
