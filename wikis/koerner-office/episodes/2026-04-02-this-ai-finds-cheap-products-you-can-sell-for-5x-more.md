## Source summary

Chris demonstrates using an AI sourcing agent to research, spec, and contact suppliers for a product idea: a porch/package delivery lockbox. The workflow combines trend/problem discovery, prompt-based supplier search, Amazon price comparison, tech-pack generation, and automated supplier inquiries.

## Business ideas

- AI-assisted product sourcing for ecommerce founders.
- Porch-piracy/package lockbox brand or local resale test.
- Product-research service that turns a rough idea into supplier options and a tech pack.
- Marketplace/Amazon/Shopify launch around sourced products.

## Customer segments mentioned

- ecommerce founders with product ideas but no sourcing experience
- people selling through Facebook Marketplace, Amazon, or Shopify
- homeowners worried about package theft
- product designers needing manufacturers or tech packs

## Acquisition channels

- Google Trends/problem-led product selection

## Operational steps

1. Start from a visible problem/trend such as porch piracy.
2. Prompt the sourcing agent with desired materials, look, locking/weatherproof constraints, and product function.
3. Ask ChatGPT to improve the sourcing prompt if needed.
4. Compare supplier options with Amazon listings and likely retail prices.
5. Generate or request a tech pack/spec sheet.
6. Send supplier inquiries and let the tool auto-follow-up where appropriate.
7. Test through a low-friction channel before scaling inventory.

## Tools / AI workflows

- Axio-style AI sourcing agent
- ChatGPT for prompt improvement
- Alibaba/AliExpress-style supplier data
- Amazon comparison searches
- tech-pack generation and automated supplier inquiry replies

## Caveats and claims to verify

- Supplier quality, certifications, shipping, duties, MOQ, and lead times can erase apparent margins.
- Amazon comparisons do not prove sell-through or ad economics.
- Product safety, locks, weatherproofing, and porch theft claims need validation.
- Marketplace tests do not replace full landed-cost analysis.
