## Source summary

Chris builds a missed-call text-back workflow in GoHighLevel for a tree-service example, then shows how to dogfood the same workflow with ringless voicemails and scraped phone numbers to find local businesses that need speed-to-leed automation.

## Business ideas

- Missed-call text-back setup for local service businesses.
- White-label AI receptionist/CRM workflow sold monthly.
- Ringless-voicemail prospecting campaign that triggers the same missed-call demo.
- Niche packages for tree service, plumbing, HVAC, and other urgent local services.

## Customer segments mentioned

- tree trimmers
- plumbers, HVAC companies, and home-service providers
- small businesses missing calls after hours
- owners without employees or a formal CRM

- No exact selling price is given, but the workflow is framed as a resellable recurring-revenue AI service.

## Acquisition channels

- scrape local business phone numbers
- ringless voicemail drops
- dogfood missed-call workflow so prospects experience the product
- demonstrate first-response data and missed-call problem

## Operational steps

1. Build a workflow triggered by an incoming missed call.
2. Assign/contact-tag the lead inside the CRM.
3. Send a short text asking what the caller needs.
4. Ask a bounded number of qualifying questions and wait long enough between replies.
5. Swap niche-specific prompt text for each business type.
6. Duplicate the workflow for prospecting and feed it calls from ringless voicemail drops.

## Tools / AI workflows

- GoHighLevel workflows, CRM tags, lead stages, and AI conversation steps
- Claude to trim prompt/question text under word limits
- scraped Google/local lists cleaned in spreadsheets
- ringless voicemail service (example: Slybroadcast)

## Caveats and claims to verify

- Ringless voicemail, SMS, and outbound campaigns have compliance constraints.
- AI responses need guardrails so they do not overpromise or harass leads.
- Local businesses need calendar/booking handoff configured correctly.
- “57% of sales goes to whoever responds first” should be independently verified before reuse.
