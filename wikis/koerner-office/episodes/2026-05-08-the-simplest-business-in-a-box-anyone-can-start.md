## Source summary

Mark presents a simplified nitro/iced-coffee cart model: shelf-stable kegged coffee, few menu choices, easy setup, low spoilage, and event/location arbitrage. The lesson is less about premium coffee culture and more about stripping a concession business down to speed, durability, and accessible operations.

## Business ideas

- Nitro/iced coffee cart at events, gas stations, car shows, boat shows, air shows, gyms, and supercharger locations.
- Build carts for operators and split profits.
- Franchise/licensing-style "business in a box" for underemployed operators.
- Expand from coffee to carbonated juices or other simple tapped beverages.

## Customer segments mentioned

- event attendees in hot/weather-exposed settings
- gas-station/car-wash/supercharger customers
- divorced parents or veterans seeking flexible local income
- location owners who benefit from extra traffic
- event organizers and concession operators

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Event examples include $8,000 net profit in two days and $400 profit at a small 80-person event.
- Cost per cup discussed around $3 with sale price around $8.50, leaving roughly $5 gross profit.
- Operator frames many events as $100-$400/hour profit opportunities.

## Acquisition channels

- securing permission from high-traffic locations
- partnerships with gas stations, car washes, gyms, and event venues
- simple signage such as "iced coffee"
- piggybacking on existing crowds and adjacent vendors

## Operational steps

1. Source kegged/shelf-stable coffee or similar tapped beverage.
2. Build a simple cart that avoids expensive espresso equipment.
3. Reduce menu complexity to a few clear options.
4. Test locations and events with low downside because inventory does not spoil quickly.
5. Track which signage, geography, season, and crowd type convert.
6. Add operators/partners once the location economics work.

## Tools / AI workflows

- kegged coffee/nitro equipment
- FedEx Office PVC signs
- Stripe or Square for card/watch payments
- simple carts rather than complex trucks

## Caveats and claims to verify

- Permitting varies by municipality and location.
- Revenue depends heavily on weather, crowd, season, and signage.
- Liability and food-service compliance should be checked locally.
- Self-reported event profits need verification.
