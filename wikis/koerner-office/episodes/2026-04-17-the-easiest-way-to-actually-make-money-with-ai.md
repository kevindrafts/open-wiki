## Source summary

Corey describes a paid AI-assessment service for small businesses: interview the owner about daily workflows, turn the transcript into a short report of quick wins and tools, then use the report as a paid diagnostic and upsell path for implementation work.

## Business ideas

- $1,000 AI workflow assessments for local SMBs.
- Voice-agent intake that interviews owners and produces a structured transcript.
- Follow-on implementation projects: CRM setup, Zapier/Make automations, custom GPTs, knowledge systems, and speed-to-lead agents.
- Paid or free local AI meetups and office hours as lead generation.

## Customer segments mentioned

- five-to-50-employee small businesses
- realtors and brokerages
- business brokers
- wedding venue operators
- barber schools and other local operators
- owners with teams and repeated manual admin work

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Assessments moved from free pilots to $200, then $500, then $1,000.
- Process optimization is framed as a $3K-$5K upsell; automations as roughly $1K-$3K.
- Example Zapier automation sold for $1,500.
- CRM or larger implementation work can become $3K-$5K+ proposals.

## Acquisition channels

- local AI meetups through Luma/Meetup or partner offices
- realtor-office workshops and office hours
- door knocking local businesses
- warm-network/free pilot audits for testimonials
- authority created by hosting the event, not by having a huge audience

## Operational steps

1. Interview the owner by Zoom or a voice agent; avoid high-friction Loom/screen-recording asks.
2. Clean the transcript and ask Claude to identify pain points and off-the-shelf tools.
3. Build a concise report with quick wins, effort/impact matrix, recommended tools, and a short implementation plan.
4. Walk the owner through the report live.
5. Quantify time/revenue impact and propose one narrow upsell.
6. Specialize in one implementation type before trying to sell every AI service.

## Tools / AI workflows

- Claude for transcript analysis, tool research, and report drafting
- voice agent intake (example built on Retell AI)
- Gamma for report/slideshow formatting
- Fathom/Fireflies-style meeting copilots
- DashThis, GoHighLevel, Zapier, Make, Asana, custom GPTs

## Caveats and claims to verify

- Do not sell generic “AI”; tie recommendations to saved hours or revenue.
- Voice-agent/report automation still needs human review before delivery.
- Business owners may have broken processes that should be fixed before automating.
- AI expertise claims should be bounded; the guest emphasizes being one step ahead, not pretending to be a deep expert.
