## Summary

This episode breaks down a concrete floor-coating business that reportedly reached ~$60k revenue in its first full month. The durable pattern is a premium [[local-home-service-businesses]] playbook: visually satisfying service, high-ticket residential jobs, Meta ads, fast lead response, simple crew operations, reviews/referrals, and AI-assisted ad copy.

## Concrete business ideas

- Premium residential concrete coating / epoxy-flake garage floor service
- patios, porches, walkways, shop floors, warehouse floors, and commercial grind-and-seal jobs
- second-crew or nearby-city expansion after proof in one local market

## Customer segments

- older homeowners, especially men 50+
- higher-income homeowners with 2-4 car garages
- organized-garage / home-improvement buyers
- commercial/light-industrial customers like warehouses, shops, and churches

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Standard coating: about $8/sq. ft.
- 500 sq. ft. garage ≈ $4,000
- hard material cost discussed around ~$2/sq. ft.
- monthly overhead cited around ~$7,300
- planning model: 7,000 sq. ft./month × $8 = $56k/month
- 10% non-refundable deposit
- content-moving add-on around $1/sq. ft.
- claimed profit over 50%, but self-reported

## Acquisition channels

- Meta/Facebook ads, especially process and before/after videos
- Google Business Profile and reviews
- referral program with cash incentive
- church/community/personal network early customers
- local service ads selectively

## Operational playbook

1. Test on own garage.
2. Set up supplier, insurance, equipment, branding, and website.
3. Run visual Meta ads in a local radius.
4. Respond immediately to leads.
5. Prefer in-person estimates.
6. Take deposit and schedule jobs.
7. Grind and prep the concrete.
8. Apply base coat, broadcast flakes, scrape/blow excess, apply top coat.
9. Collect final payment.
10. Ask for reviews and referrals.

## AI/tool workflow

- Claude for ad headlines, descriptions, and copy.
- Prompt includes business, location, target customer, and ad-copy request.
- Quick website/no-code testing before full equipment investment is suggested.

## Caveats / claims to verify

- Financial claims are self-reported.
- Licensing, insurance, chemical handling, safety, and worker classification vary by market.
- Surface prep mistakes can create warranty/rework risk.
- Weather and seasonality matter.
- Startup cost depends on whether equipment is rented/tested or purchased professionally.
