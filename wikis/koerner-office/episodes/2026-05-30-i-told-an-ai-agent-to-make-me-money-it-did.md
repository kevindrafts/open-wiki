## Summary

Chris Koerner and Brandon Doyle brainstorm businesses an AI agent could launch, then revisit the results a month later. Two experiments work: a personalized bedtime-story email product reaches a few hundred dollars in monthly recurring revenue, and a local-business website/direct-mail campaign reportedly reaches a little over $8k MRR. The episode is a strong source for [[ai-agent-operated-businesses]] and [[local-website-direct-mail]].

## Concrete business ideas

- AI-personalized bedtime stories delivered by email each night.
- AI agent that finds local businesses, builds demo websites, and supports outbound sales.
- Website/marketing agency using free prebuilt websites as a hook.
- Local business prospecting workflow that combines AI research, direct mail, QR codes, and phone follow-up.
- Marketplace/eBay arbitrage agent; discussed but deprioritized.

## Customer segments

- parents of young children willing to pay for personalized bedtime content
- local service businesses in Utah, including landscaping, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, and similar categories
- businesses with no website or weak websites
- small businesses that already buy or might buy marketing services

## Pricing / revenue clues

- Bedtime-story product: $9/month for one child; discussed moving multi-child plan from $24 to $19.
- Bedtime-story product: guest reports a little over $300 MRR from roughly 33-34 customers after about $400 ad spend.
- Local website/direct-mail experiment: guest reports a little over $8k MRR.
- Local website experiment: roughly 17-20 customers, around $400/month average, with some plans up to about $1,200/month.
- Direct mail cost: a little more than $1/postcard; roughly $400 for the campaign, plus about $10-$20 in token cost.

## Acquisition channels

- Meta/Facebook/Instagram ads for the bedtime-story product.
- Human founder video ad outperformed AI/static creative in the small bedtime-story test.
- AI-generated list of 350 local businesses from Google Business/Profile data.
- Personalized demo websites hosted on test URLs.
- QR-code postcards via Lob that link each business owner to a prebuilt website.
- Sales calls, especially to owners who scanned the QR code.

## Operational playbook

### Bedtime-story product

1. Use an agent to build a simple signup/account flow.
2. Collect child profile details such as name, favorite animal/color, and preferred themes.
3. Send a personalized story by email at a predictable nightly time.
4. Start with a low monthly price and a free trial/free sample story.
5. Test human and AI-generated ad creative.
6. Improve story quality and consider a human/founder-led editorial layer before scaling.

### Local website/direct-mail campaign

1. Ask the agent to find several hundred local businesses in a target region.
2. Pull business name, owner/contact name where available, email, phone, and business category from public listings.
3. Generate simple websites from public business data and host them on test URLs.
4. Generate a QR-coded postcard for each business that points to its demo site.
5. Mail postcards through a direct-mail API such as Lob.
6. Track scans and prioritize calls to owners who scanned.
7. Offer the website for free if the owner hires the agency for ongoing marketing.
8. Track which segments, existing-site status, and scan behavior produce closes in future batches.

## AI/tool workflow

- OpenClaw agent accessed through iMessage and running on a Mac Mini.
- Connected to Google Docs, Gmail, Google Drive, and iMessage for delivery of results.
- Lower-cost models used to keep mass website-generation token costs low.
- Lob for postcard/direct-mail API and delivery analytics.
- Meta ads for customer acquisition on the bedtime-story product.
- Possible future integrations: Replit/Lovable-style builders, GoDaddy/domain purchase, CRM, and scan-triggered sales workflows.

## Caveats / claims to verify

- All revenue, scan, close-rate, and cost figures are guest-reported.
- Scraping/contact gathering and outreach must comply with platform terms, privacy law, telemarketing/SMS/email rules, and local direct-mail rules.
- Free prebuilt websites can create trust, but quality and truthfulness matter; avoid implying ownership or endorsement.
- The team did not fully track closes by whether a prospect already had a website.
- The bedtime-story product should avoid copyrighted source material and should have safety/quality review for children's content.
