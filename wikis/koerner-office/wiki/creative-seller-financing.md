# Creative Seller Financing

Creative seller financing uses trust, value creation, and negotiated terms to acquire assets with less cash upfront. Structures include seller notes, graduated payments, balloon payments, partial seller carrybacks, future-upside participation, master leases, lease-to-own, subject-to debt, collateral in lieu of cash, and delayed down payments.

## Patterns

- Trust-building is the core tactic; terms work better when the seller believes the buyer will improve the asset.
- Public proof and reputation risk can substitute for some missing track record.
- Match the structure to the seller's real constraint: price, monthly income, speed, risk, taxes, or future upside.
- Use value-add plans to justify seller participation in upside.
- Avoid emotional attachment; the numbers and risk allocation should determine whether to proceed.

## Example structures

- Low down payment plus monthly seller note and balloon.
- Graduated payments that rise after the buyer proves execution.
- Bank loan plus seller carryback for the missing down payment.
- Master lease while improving occupancy before purchase.
- Subject-to existing debt where legal and acceptable.

## Caveats

Creative finance can create major legal, tax, lender, due-on-sale, foreclosure, securities, and personal-guarantee risks. Professional counsel is required.
