# Agricultural Drone Services

Agricultural drone services use heavy-lift drones to spray crops, rangeland, or hard-to-reach fields where ground rigs, airplanes, or helicopters are expensive, unavailable, or less precise. The Koerner Office example shows a broader drone-business flywheel: service revenue, content, operator directories, equipment sales, training, and farm demos reinforcing each other.

## Pattern

A drone-services business often combines:

- visually compelling demonstrations
- local regulatory and licensing competence
- expensive equipment with high utilization during short seasons
- direct selling to farmers rather than waiting for search demand
- clear comparison to existing alternatives: ground rigs, airplanes, helicopters
- adjacent monetization from training, equipment, and content

This overlaps with [[boring-business-ideas]], [[local-home-service-businesses]], and [[sales-and-distribution-playbooks]].

## Applications

- crop spraying where ground equipment cannot enter wet or tight fields
- specialty crops, rugged acres, edges, and power-line-obstructed fields
- rangeland/invasive maintenance programs
- deer recovery and thermal-drone services as adjacent/off-season businesses

## Operating principles

1. Show farmers the drone and droplet/application quality in person.
2. Get landowner permission before flight demonstrations.
3. Hold required remote-pilot and pesticide-applicator licenses.
4. Let farmers choose the chemicals until you have agronomic expertise.
5. Price relative to airplane/ground alternatives and difficulty of the acres.
6. Treat seasonality as a constraint: work hard during the window and manage cash.
