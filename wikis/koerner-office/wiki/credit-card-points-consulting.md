# Credit Card Points Consulting

Credit card points consulting is a high-margin advisory service that helps business owners earn and redeem rewards from spending they already do. In the Koerner Office example, the wedge is not generic financial advice; it is a narrow “points strategy and redemption help” service for owners with meaningful monthly expenses or large unused point balances.

## Pattern

A points-consulting offer usually includes:

- audit current business and personal card setup
- map spending categories to reward opportunities
- simplify card recommendations around the owner's goals
- help transfer and redeem points for flights or hotels
- charge flat fees for strategy calls or award-travel research
- publish client stories to build trust and inbound demand

This connects to [[one-person-businesses]] because fulfillment can start as expert labor, and to [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]] because AI and award-search tools can accelerate research while a human verifies feasibility.

## Example from Koerner Office

From `episodes/2026-05-16-the-credit-card-side-hustle-no-one-talks-about.md`: Colin describes charging $875 for a one-time business-owner strategy package and also charging flat fees for award-travel research. He says LinkedIn content generated most clients, with an email list as a backup channel.

## Customer segments

- local business owners using low-reward cards
- entrepreneurs with high ad spend, shipping, inventory, or operating expenses
- families with large point balances who want premium travel
- business owners who want a human to validate a strategy rather than relying on AI alone

## Tool stack

- Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini for learning and research assistance
- points blogs/newsletters such as Frequent Miler, The Points Guy, Daily Drop, and 10xTravel
- award-search tools such as seats.aero
- a content/newsletter archive for recurring examples and proof

## Caveats

- Credit-card recommendations can involve credit, tax, compliance, affiliate-disclosure, and suitability issues.
- Points programs devalue; holding points is risky relative to earning and redeeming with a plan.
- AI can produce unrealistic redemption suggestions, so human verification is central to the service.
- Public artifacts should avoid individualized financial advice.
