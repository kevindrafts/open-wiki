# AI Agent-Operated Businesses

AI agent-operated businesses use an always-on AI assistant to perform parts of the entrepreneurial workflow: research, prospecting, building, content generation, light operations, reporting, and handoff to a human closer/operator. Koerner Office examples show agents as leverage, not magic: the best results still pair agent work with narrow prompts, existing tools, human review, and distribution.

## Pattern

A useful agent-operated business usually has:

- a narrow job with clear inputs and outputs
- access to the tools/data needed to complete the job
- a low-cost way to run repeated tasks
- a human review or sales handoff at the risky step
- a monetization path that does not depend on the agent being perfect

This connects to [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]] because the agent often performs fulfillment or research inside a service business, and to [[one-person-ai-agencies]] because a solo operator can use agents to look larger than they are.

## Operating principles

1. Put the agent where the operator already works: iMessage, Telegram, Slack, email, or another daily interface.
2. Use cheaper models for high-volume low-risk tasks and stronger models for strategy or quality-sensitive steps.
3. Keep humans in the loop for compliance, money, medical/legal/financial content, and final sales conversations.
4. Make the output concrete: a spreadsheet, demo website, story email, proposal, or alert.
5. Track the funnel so the next run can improve.

## Open questions

- What compliance boundaries apply to scraping, contacting, or auto-generating content for prospects?
- Which tasks should remain human-led for trust even when an agent can technically do them?
