# White-Label Vertical SaaS

White-label vertical SaaS is the Koerner Office pattern of taking an existing software platform, packaging it for a narrow industry, and selling the bundle as a niche-specific operating system. The advantage is speed: the operator can focus on customer pain, positioning, distribution, templates, training, and support instead of building core infrastructure from scratch.

## Pattern

A strong white-label vertical SaaS offer usually combines:

- a narrow buyer group with repeat operational pain
- existing software that can be reskinned, configured, or templated
- one wedge feature that feels like a painkiller, not a generic CRM
- training, community, templates, or services that make the software sticky
- recurring pricing that covers platform fees and support
- distribution through niche trust, creators, associations, or direct outreach

This overlaps with [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]] when AI helps with research, configuration, support, onboarding, or feature-gap analysis, and with [[sales-and-distribution-playbooks]] because the model often depends more on niche distribution than novel code.

## Operating principles

1. Start with a niche you understand or can partner into.
2. Sell the urgent workflow first; do not lead with a generic feature list.
3. Use reviews and complaints to find missing features or onboarding pain.
4. Add training, templates, community, or implementation to reduce churn.
5. Charge monthly unless the lifetime price fully accounts for long-term costs.
6. Avoid misleading “lifetime” offers when the backend costs recur monthly.
7. Treat white-label software as a distribution and support business, not passive income.

## Open questions

- Which niche communities have trusted creators who would revenue-share a white-label tool?
- Which platform costs and support obligations make a niche unattractive?
- Which regulated niches require licensing, disclosures, or professional review?
- Which feature gaps can be validated with a manual service before a software bundle?
