# Direct-Mail Co-op Advertising

Direct-mail co-op advertising sells multiple non-competing local businesses small ad spaces on one shared postcard, then mails the card to targeted homeowner routes through USPS Every Door Direct Mail. The model turns a large postage/printing bill into a sponsor marketplace: advertisers split distribution costs, while the operator designs the card, coordinates categories, and tracks response.

## Pattern

A typical run has:

- selected EDDM routes matched to advertiser categories
- one advertiser per industry to create urgency/scarcity
- front-side inventory priced to cover print/postage
- free ad design and QR tracking bundled into the purchase
- strong local offers rather than generic branding
- follow-up to collect results/case studies

This connects to [[local-service-lead-generation]], [[local-website-direct-mail]], and [[sales-and-distribution-playbooks]].

## Best-fit advertisers

- home services: plumbing, roofing, landscaping, junk hauling, carpet/window cleaning
- local restaurants, bakeries, and retail with redeemable offers
- realtors with home-value lead magnets
- businesses already using physical local ads such as yard signs

## Operating principles

1. Sell exposure and local reach; do not guarantee ROI.
2. Encourage simple, strong offers that can be tracked.
3. Use dynamic QR codes or unique URLs/phone numbers when possible.
4. Confirm USPS bundling and print specs before ordering thousands of cards.
5. Treat post-campaign follow-up as the next sales asset.
6. Re-offer first right of refusal to prior advertisers for adjacent routes.
