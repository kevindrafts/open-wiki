# One-Person AI Agencies

One-person AI agencies sell concrete business outcomes while using AI tools to reduce discovery, proposal writing, implementation, content creation, CRM setup, automation, and ongoing support work. The Koerner Office pattern is not to pitch vague AI transformation, but to find a real pain point, build a narrow useful solution, and price around the client value.

## Pattern

A solo AI agency usually starts with:

- a warm-network or local-business conversation
- a recorded discovery call
- AI-assisted analysis of the client's pain points
- a proposal tied to revenue, savings, or operational relief
- implementation in existing tools such as CRM, email, forms, content systems, or agent workflows
- human review for sensitive outputs

This links to [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]] because the agency is a service wrapper around AI-enabled fulfillment, and to [[ai-agent-operated-businesses]] because agents can increasingly perform parts of discovery, buildout, and monitoring.

## Offer shapes

- AI business assessment: free, low-cost, and higher-ticket diagnostic tiers.
- CRM setup/reactivation for stale customer lists.
- Voice agent or chatbot setup for service businesses.
- Content-generation system using a client's existing knowledge base.
- Product launch automation for a specific new service.
- Grant research/drafting workflow for nonprofits.
- Website/lead-generation package for local businesses.

## Sales principles

1. Start with people who already trust you.
2. Ask questions and listen before explaining AI capabilities.
3. Record the conversation and use AI to synthesize needs, objections, and possible solutions.
4. Sell the outcome, not the tool.
5. Use one useful build to unlock follow-on work.
6. Ask for referrals once a client sees value.
7. Narrow into a repeatable niche only after revenue reveals what buyers want.

## Caveats

- Guest-reported revenue should not be treated as verified performance.
- Medical, financial, grant, legal, and regulated marketing workflows need human review.
- Warm-network outreach should preserve trust and avoid MLM-style pressure.
- Solo agencies can become unfocused; a repeatable offer or niche is needed for scale.
