# Fractional AI Implementation Services

Fractional AI implementation services sell ongoing help to mid-market companies that know AI matters but do not know what to install, train, automate, or govern. The Koerner Office examples position the offer above one-off AI audits: strategy plus process transformation plus employee education, often sold to CEOs as a way to avoid missing the AI shift.

## Pattern

A fractional AI implementation offer usually includes:

- AI readiness or workflow diagnosis
- executive strategy and roadmap work
- installation of repeatable systems, dashboards, automations, and agent workflows
- employee training that makes internal teams capable of using the tools
- ongoing retainer support rather than a one-time report
- ROI framing tied to revenue, headcount avoidance, speed, or productivity

This is related to [[ai-audits-for-small-businesses]], [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]], and [[one-person-ai-agencies]].

## Target buyers

Best-fit buyers are often larger than microbusinesses: companies with enough revenue, employees, and operational complexity to justify a five-figure check, but not enough internal AI capability to move quickly on their own.

## Operating principles

1. Sell to the CEO's fear of falling behind, but keep claims grounded in specific workflows.
2. Avoid becoming only a custom dev shop; maintenance can swamp a nontechnical founder.
3. Teach clients to build/modify internal tools so the engagement compounds.
4. Start with motivating projects that show nontechnical employees what is possible.
5. Package strategy, transformation, and education together.
6. Use public posting and referrals to make trust travel faster than cold outbound.

## Common deliverables

- CEO dashboards connected to company systems
- AI-assisted scraping/research/reporting workflows
- sales and CRM enrichment automation
- internal training cohorts for vibe coding and agent use
- operational systems for marketing, sales, and admin processes
