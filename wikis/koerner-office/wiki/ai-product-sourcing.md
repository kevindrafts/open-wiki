# AI Product Sourcing

AI product sourcing uses agents and LLMs to turn a rough product idea into supplier searches, specifications, tech packs, price comparisons, and manufacturer inquiries. It can help ecommerce operators move faster, but the AI output must be validated against landed cost, quality, safety, demand, and channel economics.

## Pattern

A sourcing workflow usually includes:

- trend/problem discovery
- a detailed natural-language product brief
- supplier search across marketplaces/manufacturers
- product specification or tech-pack generation
- retail price comparison and margin estimate
- inquiry automation and supplier follow-up
- small-channel testing before large inventory buys

This connects to [[resale-and-liquidation]], [[tiny-tools-and-micro-apps]], and [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]].

## Channels

- Facebook Marketplace for fast local listing tests
- Amazon for demand and competition checks
- Shopify plus paid ads for branded ecommerce
- supplier marketplaces such as Alibaba/AliExpress-style catalogs

## Operating principles

1. Use AI to create options, not to make final buying decisions.
2. Calculate landed cost, MOQ, shipping, duties, and defect risk.
3. Request samples and verify claims before scale orders.
4. Treat marketplace price gaps as hypotheses until sell-through is proven.
5. Validate safety/regulatory requirements for physical goods.
