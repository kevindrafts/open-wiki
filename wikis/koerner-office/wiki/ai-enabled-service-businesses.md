# AI-Enabled Service Businesses

AI-enabled service businesses package a narrow customer outcome and use AI to reduce research, fulfillment, admin, content, prospecting, or operations work. The strongest Koerner Office pattern is not “sell AI” generically, but sell a concrete business result where AI makes a previously manual service faster, cheaper, or possible for a solo operator.

## Pattern

A good AI-enabled service business usually has:

- a buyer who already pays for the outcome
- messy inputs that make pure software difficult
- repeatable workflow steps
- enough margin for human review
- a clear before/after result
- a simple first customer acquisition path

This links to [[boring-business-ideas]] because many examples are unglamorous but practical, and to [[one-person-businesses]] because AI can let one operator fulfill work that previously required a small team.

## Operating principles

1. Sell the business result, not “AI implementation.”
2. Start narrow enough that the workflow can be checked manually.
3. Keep a human review layer for money, customer communication, legal, or migration work.
4. Use existing tools first; avoid waiting for perfect autonomous agents.
5. Build repeatable fulfillment before turning it into software.
6. Treat model improvements as margin expansion, not as the entire business.
