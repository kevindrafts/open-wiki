# AI Audits for Small Businesses

AI audits for small businesses are paid assessments that identify where AI, automation, and off-the-shelf tools can save time or increase revenue inside an existing company. The durable opportunity is not the one-off report; it is using the report to uncover concrete implementation work such as CRM setup, automations, custom GPTs, speed-to-lead systems, and knowledge bases.

## Pattern

A strong AI-audit offer usually includes:

- a low-friction owner interview rather than asking the client to install unfamiliar tools
- transcript analysis to find repeated pain points
- a short effort/impact matrix
- tool recommendations with clear installation steps
- a quick-win plan that makes the advice actionable
- quantified savings or revenue impact
- one narrow implementation upsell

This overlaps with [[ai-enabled-service-businesses]], [[one-person-ai-agencies]], and [[home-service-ai-lead-capture]].

## Implementation menu

- Process optimization before automation.
- Simple Zapier/Make builds for handoff, formatting, routing, or CRM hygiene.
- CRM setup and migration for businesses still operating from inboxes/spreadsheets.
- Custom GPT/knowledge systems for repeated buyer, staff, or customer questions.
- Speed-to-lead agents that respond to forms, calls, texts, or missed inquiries.
- Brand-voice/content skills for recurring marketing work.

## Acquisition channels

- local AI meetups and workshops
- realtor-office or coworking-space office hours
- door knocking SMBs while AI curiosity is high
- warm-network pilot assessments for testimonials
- local business owners with 5-50 employees and obvious manual workflows

## Operating principles

1. Sell saved time or revenue, not “AI implementation.”
2. Charge for real diagnostic work unless intentionally collecting testimonials.
3. Use existing tools before custom software.
4. Fix bad processes before automating them.
5. Specialize in one repeatable upsell early.
6. Keep human review in the report and implementation loop.
