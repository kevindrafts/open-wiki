# Live-Selling Liquidation

Live-selling liquidation businesses buy overstock, returns, or deal inventory and sell it through real-time auction or shopping streams on platforms such as Whatnot and TikTok Shop. The edge is a combination of buying discipline, perceived value, entertaining presentation, community trust, and fast fulfillment.

## Pattern

A strong live-selling operation has:

- reliable liquidation or overstock sourcing
- products with high perceived value and easy demonstration/testing
- a streamer with energy, category interest, and trust
- auctions or bundles that make buying feel urgent
- tracked profit per hour, not just gross sales
- packing/shipping workflow ready before scaling

This overlaps with [[resale-and-liquidation]], [[sales-and-distribution-playbooks]], and [[one-person-businesses]].

## Platform differences

- Whatnot: smaller audiences can still buy heavily because buyer intent and community are higher.
- TikTok Shop: much larger discovery surface, but viewers are often less purchase-ready and require more volume/showmanship.

## Operating principles

1. Buy with math; sell with emotion.
2. Study existing streams before sourcing inventory.
3. Start with categories that can be tested and described honestly.
4. Avoid lots where defect risk or shipping complexity overwhelms margin.
5. Use great streamers when the category depends on trust and energy.
6. Solve the shipping objection with bundles or appropriate product mix.
