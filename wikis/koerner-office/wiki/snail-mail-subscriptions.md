# Snail Mail Subscriptions

Snail mail subscriptions sell recurring tangible delight: paper goods, art, letters, recipes, stickers, stories, or niche artifacts delivered physically rather than digitally. In the Koerner Office corpus, the model benefits from the same cultural forces that make AI feel abundant and impersonal: customers value objects that feel handmade, scarce, surprising, and offline.

## Patterns

- Start with a tiny batch and let emotional response guide scale.
- Short-form video can sell the making process while preserving the surprise.
- Unboxing posts create organic referral loops.
- Waitlists should clearly state price to avoid low-intent signups.
- Labor batching matters: stamping, folding, stuffing, packing, and fulfillment can become the bottleneck.
- Archives of past letters can monetize late-arriving fans.

## Possible niches

Stationery, junk journaling, recipes, local history, classic cars, hunting, golf, DIY projects, run clubs, realtor touchpoints, financial-advisor client gifts, and serialized fiction.
