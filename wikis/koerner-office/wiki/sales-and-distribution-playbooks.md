# Sales and Distribution Playbooks

Sales and distribution playbooks in the Koerner Office corpus emphasize practical routes to customers: proof-first selling, local partnerships, comment-to-DM funnels, physical gifts, referrals, marketplaces, and piggybacking on existing traffic. The repeated pattern is to reduce sales friction by showing results, borrowing attention, or making the outreach more memorable than generic cold email.

## Recurring plays

- **Proof before pitch:** rank-and-rent websites route free calls before asking for payment.
- **Borrowed traffic:** farmers markets, shopping centers, venues, and events already have buyers.
- **Comment-to-lead systems:** content captures emails through automated DMs and lead magnets.
- **Physical-world attention:** cakes and corporate gifts stand out as AI makes digital outreach noisier.
- **Referral loops:** gifting and local services grow when customers publicly show results or tell peers.

## When to use which

Use proof-first sales when fulfillment can be done before closing. Use borrowed traffic when demand is obvious but audience-building would take too long. Use physical gifts when customer LTV justifies high-touch prospecting. Use automated content funnels when the operator can consistently publish.
