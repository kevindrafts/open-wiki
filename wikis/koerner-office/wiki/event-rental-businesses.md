# Event Rental Businesses

Event rental businesses turn simple equipment into local experiences: backyard movies, school events, HOAs, birthday parties, weddings, dance floors, and bundled party packages. The Koerner Office pattern is to start with one rentable asset, validate demand through local marketplaces and word of mouth, then add premium bundles and adjacent upsells.

## Pattern

Strong event-rental ideas often have:

- visible before/after or experience photos
- local, time-bound demand
- equipment that can be reused across many bookings
- clear package tiers
- referrals from successful events
- natural upsells like chairs, lights, music, catering, or add-on equipment

This connects to [[boring-business-ideas]], [[one-person-businesses]], and [[sales-and-distribution-playbooks]] because the business can begin locally without software and grow through packaging and repeatable operations.

## Example: backyard movie theater rentals

From `episodes/2026-06-11-the-most-profitable-side-hustle-no-one-is-doing-yet.md`: a backyard movie setup starts with an inflatable screen and grows into premium packages with commercial screens, projectors, speakers, seating, indoor inflatable theaters, AC, lighting, carpet, and LED dance floors.

## Operating principles

1. Test demand with inexpensive equipment before buying premium gear.
2. Use local marketplaces and neighborhood networks first.
3. Photograph every good setup.
4. Upgrade based on actual failures: wind, audio, weather, heat, setup time.
5. Package by customer outcome, not by equipment list.
6. Add adjacent rentals only when customers already ask for them.

## Risks

- weather and wind
- insurance/liability
- equipment failure
- electrical safety
- public-performance licensing for movies
- seasonality
- imported equipment quality and warranty risk
