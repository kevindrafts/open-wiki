## Longer version

I started a public experiment called Open Wiki.

The idea is simple: lots of useful business/startup/dev knowledge is trapped inside long YouTube videos and podcasts. Transcripts make it searchable, but they do not make it compound.

So instead of dumping transcripts into a folder, I’m turning public sources into a living markdown wiki:

- one source note per episode
- durable topic pages
- timestamped citations back to the original videos
- caveats for claims that need verification
- reusable patterns across sources

First corpus: Koerner Office.

The first few videos already produced three different business patterns:

### 1. AI-enabled service businesses

Examples: bookkeeping cleanup, legacy software migration, productized one-person agencies, personalized outreach, and self-improving digital assets.

The pattern is not “sell AI.” It is: sell a concrete outcome and use AI to make fulfillment faster or cheaper.

### 2. Food/beverage concessions

The lemonade episode is less about lemonade and more about simple event-based distribution:

- clear offer
- strong margins
- farmers markets/events
- catering
- cart rentals
- venue partnerships

### 3. Raw land wholesaling

A builder-first version of land wholesaling:

- identify what builders want
- find matching off-market lots
- get a contract below the builder’s buy price
- assign the deal through title

The repo is here: https://github.com/kevindrafts/open-wiki

I’m starting with Koerner Office videos from 2026, then I’ll add more sources/channels over time.

The goal: source-backed public wikis that turn internet rabbit holes into useful operating knowledge.
